User observations for grocery stores

Project overview

Description

Our assignment was to create a general customer journey for grocery stores. The first step was doing research and creating a persona for the chosen target group .This phase of the process included both quantitive and qualitative research. After creating a persona the research phase included user-observations. When the observations had been conducted we created a customer journey based on these.

Process
  • Working agile
  • Create persona
  • User Observations
  • Create customer journey
Goal

To get a deeper understanding of a customers experience shopping at a grocery store and finding what motivates and frustrates them during a visit.

Scope

2 weeks

Survey & Persona

Survey

The first step of this project was to create a simple persona for our chosen target group, which was people with a limited economical budget. To do this we made a survey with google forms and collected responses form 15 people, which gave us a lot of information to create a persona. We asked questions regarding personal demographical facts, their grocery shopping habits and their typical grocery store in order to get a deeper understanding of their needs, motivations and frustrations.

Persona

When we had collected and analyzed all the information from our surveys we created a persona for our specified target group.

User Observations

The observation took place on site in different grocery stores and before the observation we all conducted an interview with the test subjects to gain insight into their expectations of the grocery shopping, what needs they had and what they were planning to buy. We also asked questions to the subjects to give us further information to fill our persona with.

During the observation we accompanied the test subjects in the store and documented it by filming. We also asked them to think out loud throughout the whole store visit and asked them questions when needed in order to get a better understanding of their experience during the store visit.

Creating Customer Journey

When all the people in the group had completed our observations, we compiled our data to gain insight into what similarities there were for all the people we studied. We collected information about what problems arose, what activities were positive or negative and what the overall experience were for the test subjects.

To get a good overview of all the test subjects’ experiences, we all created a simplified customer journey for each of the people we studied. This made it easier for us to find similarities between their experiences and for us to create a combined general customer journey.

When we had summarized all the similarities and differences between our observations, we combined them all into one general customer journey map.

What I've Learned

Working agile sparks efficiency

Working agile is a great way to make the working process more efficient and effective, since we divide different tasks among the group and work better as a team.

Asking the right questions

When creating a survey it can be very hard to formulate questions to get the information that you want without priming the user. Knowing what the purpose of asking each question is, however, helps you a lot when deciding and formulating questions.

Customer journeys are a great addition to a user persona

Personas are a great tool for gaining empathy and an understanding for the user. However, they are not always super helpful for a UX-designer in themselves, since they mostly provide demographic facts and not a lot of information regarding user behavior. By complementing a persona with a customer journey you gain a lot more insight into how the user interacts and behaves in a realistic setting, which is very useful for both a UX-designer and other parties of a company.
